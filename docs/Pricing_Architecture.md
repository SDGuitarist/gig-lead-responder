## PACKAGE TIERS

### Signature Package — $1,500
**Coverage:** Ceremony + Cocktail Hour (2-2.5 hours total)

**Includes:**
- Pre-event consultation (phone or video, 15-30 minutes)
- Custom repertoire design based on client preferences
- Coordination with venue and planner
- Professional sound system (when venue requires)
- All setup/breakdown
- Attire appropriate to venue
- Post-event follow-up

**Ideal for:** Couples who want ceremony and cocktail hour handled completely, with seamless transition between moments.

---

### Extended Package — $2,200
**Coverage:** Ceremony + Cocktail Hour + Dinner Background (3.5-4 hours total)

**Includes:** Everything in Signature, plus:
- Dinner background music (volume-calibrated for conversation)
- Natural transition from cocktail to dinner ambiance
- Extended repertoire curation

**Ideal for:** Couples who want music throughout the pre-reception portion of the evening, ending before DJ/dancing takes over.

---

### Full Evening Package — $2,800
**Coverage:** Ceremony through Reception (5+ hours)

**Includes:** Everything in Extended, plus:
- Reception coverage (background during dinner, featured moments as requested)
- Coordination with DJ for smooth handoff
- Extended performance hours
- Flexibility for timeline changes

**Ideal for:** Couples who want live guitar throughout the entire event, or as an alternative to DJ for more intimate receptions.

## SCOPE REDUCTION (Not Rate Reduction)

When a lead expresses budget constraints but shows genuine interest:

**Scope reduction options (maintain rate integrity):**

| Reduction | Adjusted Investment | What Changes |
|-----------|---------------------|--------------|
| Ceremony only (45-60 min) | $900 | No cocktail hour coverage |
| Cocktail hour only (1.5 hours) | $800 | No ceremony coverage |
| Shorter cocktail (1 hour) | $1,200 | Reduced from 2-hour standard |

**Language for scope reduction:**
> "My packages reflect a full-service approach. If the full Signature package is outside your budget, I can offer ceremony-only coverage at $900. That still includes consultation, custom repertoire, and complete coordination—just a focused scope."

**What NOT to do:**
- Reduce the rate for the same scope
- Offer "discount" or "deal" language
- Negotiate hourly rate downward

## WHEN TO DECLINE

Some leads are not a fit regardless of budget. Decline gracefully when:

- Event type doesn't match expertise (corporate DJ needs, band requests)
- Communication suggests difficult client (unrealistic expectations, combative tone)
- Timeline is impossible (same-week request for premium package)
- Venue is operationally incompatible (see Pacific Flow **VENUE_INTELLIGENCE.md** for red flags)

**Decline language:**
> "Based on what you've described, I don't think I'm the right fit for this one. [Brief honest reason.] I wish you a wonderful event."

**Referral when appropriate:**
> "You might try GigSalad or The Bash—you'll find talented musicians at a range of price points."

For leads that fit Pacific Flow better than Alex Guillen Music, refer internally.

## OBJECTION HANDLING

### "That's more than we expected."

> "I understand—live music is an investment. My packages reflect a full-service approach that isn't right for every couple. If budget is the primary factor, I'm happy to recommend some other musicians who might be a better fit. But if you're looking for someone who will handle the music completely—consultation, custom repertoire, coordination with your planner and venue—this is what that looks like."

### "Can you do it for less?"

> "My rates reflect the quality and reliability I bring. What I can tell you is I show up early, handle everything professionally, and your guests will have an incredible experience. If the investment doesn't feel right for what you're planning, I completely understand."

### "We found someone cheaper."

> "I understand—budget matters. The difference usually comes down to experience and the level of service. If the other option doesn't work out, feel free to reach back out."

## WHEN DOUBT CREEPS IN

Calendar gaps and competitor bookings will trigger anxiety. When that happens, remember:

**The math is on your side:**
- 30 gigs at $1,800 = 54 gigs at $1,000 = 90 gigs at $600
- You're not competing at the commodity tier anymore
- Premium positioning takes time to fill—early gaps don't mean the strategy failed

**The positioning is right:**
- 30 years and 3,000+ performances justify the floor
- Clients who book at $1,500+ are the ones you want
- Competing on price when you have your credentials is the actual losing strategy

**What to do instead of discounting:**
- Review the leads that did convert—what made them work?
- Check if marketing/visibility needs attention (not pricing)
- Reach out to planner referral contacts
- Trust the filter is working

**See:** **Level_7_Positioning** for full strategic rationale.
