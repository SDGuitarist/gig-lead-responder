## Step 10: Quality Verification

**Presence is not enough. Each component must be the best version of itself for THIS client.**

Before the Gut Check, evaluate each component against its quality standard:

### Component Quality Standards

| Component | Present (Minimum) | Excellent (Standard) |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Hook** | Opens with something relevant | Opens with the ONE detail that makes them stop scrolling. Their words, their situation, their moment—back to them. |
| **Validation** | Acknowledges them | Makes them feel seen. Names what THEY'RE doing in a way that creates ownership. They think "yes, that's exactly what I'm trying to do." |
| **Scene** | Describes the event | THEIR specific details woven into a moment they can see, hear, feel. They're standing in it. |
| **Emotion** | Implies feeling | The feeling emerges inevitably from the scene. Not told—shown. They feel it before they name it. |
| **Differentiation + Named Fear** | Names a failure mode | Names the specific thing a lesser vendor does wrong — and why that costs the client — then shows what you do differently. Fear is explicit. |
| **Logistics** | Answers questions | Preempts every question AND addresses inferred fears. Weaved into the scene naturally. No visible FAQ section. |
| **Close** | Has a next step | The next step feels like the natural conclusion. Not a sales push—an invitation. |

### Sourced Lead Quality Standards (Additional)

When Delivery Mode = Alex Sources or Hybrid, verify these additional standards:

| Component | Present (Minimum) | Excellent (Standard) |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Sourced Transparency** | Mentions that Alex coordinates musicians | Positions curation as expertise. Client understands they're hiring Alex's judgment and network, not just musicians. Never apologetic. |
| **Cultural Authenticity Signal** | Says "authentic" or "traditional" | Demonstrates specific knowledge of the tradition that proves Alex understands what makes it real vs. generic. Uses 1-2 tradition-specific terms naturally. |
| **Accountability** | Mentions coordination | Client knows Alex is their single point of contact. Logistics are handled. They don't manage musicians. |
| **Musician Quality Signal** | Says musicians are "professional" | Signals personal relationship: "musicians I know personally," "artists I've performed alongside," "musicians who inherited this tradition." |

### The Quality Test

For each component, ask: **"Is this the best version, or just a version?"**

**Example — Arc of Evening:**

| Level | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Present | "I'll adjust the music throughout the evening." |
| Good | "I shape the music in phases—lighter during cocktails, stepping back for dinner." |
| Excellent | "Warm enough to fill the silences, quiet enough that real conversation happens." |

The difference: "Present" describes what you do. "Good" shows you've thought about it. "Excellent" names what they're afraid of (silences) and what they hope for (real conversation) in one line.

**Example — Sourced Transparency:**

| Level | Example |
|-------|---------|
| Present | "I work with other musicians for this type of event." |
| Good | "I coordinate a small group of musicians I know personally." |
| Excellent | "I've been curating culturally authentic entertainment in San Diego for over 30 years. Every musician in my network has been vetted through years of working together — I don't dispatch strangers to your event." |

### The Prose Test

Read the draft aloud. Ask:

1. **Does it flow as one continuous movement?** Or do you hear the seams between components?
2. **Could this be a paragraph in a novel?** Or does it read like a business email?
3. **Does each sentence earn the next?** Or are they just stacked?

**If you hear the structure, rewrite until you don't.**

### The "Best Line" Requirement

**Every response needs at least one line that only YOU would write.**

Not a line that's professional. Not a line that's correct. A line that demonstrates understanding at a level competitors can't reach.

Examples of "best lines":
- "warm enough to fill the silences, quiet enough that real conversation happens"
- "this is the night before everything changes"
- "the kind of detail people remember without knowing why"
- "stops being 'the groom's side' and 'the bride's side' and just becomes family"

**Sourced lead best line examples:**
- "These aren't session musicians covering boleros. They inherited this music."
- "Authentic bolero trios are one of the rarest ensembles in San Diego. You found the right person to find them."
- "A full mariachi overwhelms a 40-person dinner. A 4-piece fills the room perfectly. That's the kind of call I make."
- "The requinto comes forward and the room goes still. That's not something you can fake with generic Latin musicians."

**If you can't identify your "best line," you haven't found it yet. Keep writing.**

### Revision Protocol

If any component is "present" but not "excellent":

1. **Identify the weak component**
2. **Ask: What would make this the best version for THIS client?**
3. **Rewrite that component**
4. **Re-read the whole draft for flow**
5. **Repeat until every component earns its place**

## Step 11: The Gut Check

**Final verification before delivering:**

| Check | Pass | Fail |
|-------|------|------|
| **Can they SEE it?** | A scene using THEIR specific details | Generic description or adjective list |
| **Did I validate THEM?** | Named what THEY'RE doing, not just the event | Only described the event or your service |
| **Named the fear?** | Said what burned them before, OR addressed an inferred fear from absence | Implied understanding or ignored the gap |
| **Differentiated?** | Line only you would write | Could be any vendor |
| **Preempted unasked questions?** | Sound system, COI, setup, absent fields—covered | They'll have to ask the obvious |
| **Creates relief?** | "Thank god, this person gets it" | "This one's professional too" |
| **Best line present?** | At least one line that's unmistakably excellent | All lines are merely competent |
| **Prose flows?** | Reads as one continuous movement | Structure is visible |
| **Competitor test?** | A competitor could NOT have written this | Could be any vendor with find-replace |

**Sourced lead additional checks:**

| Check | Pass | Fail |
|-------|------|------|
| **Sourced transparency?** | Curation positioned as expertise, not apology | Apologetic tone, "I won't be performing but...", or sourcing not mentioned when it should be |
| **Accountability clear?** | Client knows Alex is single point of contact | Unclear who manages the musicians or who to call |
| **Cultural authenticity signaled?** | Tradition-specific knowledge demonstrated | Generic "authentic" claim without substance |

**If ANY check fails → Rewrite before delivering.**

### The Competitor Test (Required)

**Read your draft as if a competitor wrote it.**

Ask: "Could another guitarist in San Diego have written this exact response?"

If the answer is yes—if your response is professional but interchangeable—it's not done.

**Sourced lead competitor test:** Ask: "Could a random booking agency have written this?" If a GigSalad competitor or generic entertainment agency could have dispatched the same response, you haven't demonstrated curation expertise. The response must show that Alex's knowledge of the tradition, the musicians, or the configuration is what the client is paying for — not just access to a roster.

**Signs you failed the competitor test:**
- The opening could apply to any similar event
- No line references something specific they told you
- The validation is about the event, not about THEM
- A find-and-replace of your name with a competitor's name wouldn't feel wrong

**Signs you passed:**
- The opening uses THEIR words, venue, or situation—or their silence, turned into signal
- At least one line could only come from someone who truly understood
- The validation names what THEY'RE doing, not what you're providing
- A competitor copying this would feel like plagiarism because it's so specific to THIS lead

### The Validation Test (Specific)

Find the sentence that validates THE CLIENT. Not:
- ❌ "This sounds like a beautiful evening" (validates the event)
- ❌ "Spanish guitar will be perfect for this" (validates your service)
- ❌ "What a special celebration" (generic praise)

Yes:
- ✓ "You're the one making sure tonight feels like more than dinner."
- ✓ "They're lucky to have someone who thought to do this."
- ✓ "This is the kind of detail people remember—and you made it happen."

**If you can't point to this sentence, add it.**

### The "Their Details" Test

Did you use what THEY gave you—including what they didn't give you?

- They said a venue → Did you claim credibility there?
- They said a time → Did you paint what that moment looks like?
- They said who's there → Did you put those people in the scene?
- They said indoor/outdoor → Did you address sound and setup?
- They left something blank → Did you read that absence and address it?

Generic response when they gave specifics = failure.
Generic response when they gave nothing = failure of a different kind. There is always something to work with.

### Why the Gate Has Evidence Requirements

The Verification Gate is not a vibes check. It is a mechanical check with visible evidence.

**Pre-Work Requirement:** The reasoning stage is required output, not internal thought. If the cinematic opening and validation line are not written standalone before the draft, the model is guessing. The pre-work ensures the draft is anchored to something real.

**Concern Traceability Ledger:** Every concern flagged in Step 1 (Surface Data Extraction) must be traced to a specific sentence in the draft. This includes inferred fears from absent fields. If the cell is empty, the concern was dropped. Empty cell = automatic gate failure. No exceptions. **For sourced leads:** The ledger must include "Musician identity/quality" and "Accountability/coordination" rows even if the client didn't ask — these are always-present concerns for sourced configurations.

**Scene Evidence Requirement:** Instead of self-scoring "Can they SEE it?" as PASS/FAIL without proof, quote the exact cinematic moment from the draft and classify it: structural (describes phases, logistics, arc) or cinematic (they can see/hear/feel a specific moment). Structural descriptions disguised as scenes are the most common false PASS. Forcing the classification makes the failure visible.

**Sourced Integrity Check:** When Delivery Mode = Alex Sources or Hybrid, the gate adds five additional checks specific to sourced delivery. These verify that the draft positions Alex's curation as expertise, maintains transparent but confident language about sourcing, and presents pricing without revealing internal margin structure. All five must pass for the gate to pass.

**The rule:** If you can't fill out the evidence blocks, the draft isn't ready. Rewrite until you can.

**⚠️ DO NOT OUTPUT DRAFTS UNTIL GATE STATUS = "ALL PASS"**

If any Gut Check item fails, rewrite and re-verify before producing final output.
